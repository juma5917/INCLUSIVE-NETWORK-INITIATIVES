Inclusive Network Initiative (INI) is a community-based organization that was established and registered in the year 2023 by members of groups from disability groups, orphan groups, women groups and youth groups, to support and articulate the socio-cultural and economic as well as political issues sidelining Persons with Disabilities, Youths, Women and OVCs groups through increased lobbying and advocacy activities, mainly to have a common voice in promoting their participation and involvement in the mainstream of development at all levels.
To promote and develop the capacity of children, other atmost risk population and vulnerable groups, by mainstreaming them into all community development plans, strategies and involve them in championing Disability, Health, Education, Governance issues in the community.
Igniting prowess and transforming lives of persons with disabilities, other at most risk population and vulnerable group enjoy equal right.

What We Do?

We believe that we can save more lifes with you

Healthy Food

We promote access to healthy, nutritious food by supporting sustainable farming practices, educating communities on balanced diets, and providing resources to vulnerable groups to combat hunger and malnutrition.

Pure Water

We ensure access to clean and safe water through community-led water projects, advocacy for proper sanitation, and sustainable water management practices to improve health and well-being.

Health Care

We enhance access to quality healthcare by providing education on preventive measures, supporting treatment initiatives for chronic and infectious diseases, and advocating for equitable health services for all.

Primary Education

We support inclusive and equitable primary education by providing learning resources, creating safe learning environments, and advocating for the right to education for every child, especially vulnerable and marginalized groups.

Residence Facilities

We provide safe and supportive residence facilities for vulnerable individuals, including children and persons with disabilities, ensuring access to basic amenities and a nurturing environment for growth and development.

Social Care

We offer comprehensive social care services to support vulnerable groups, including orphans, widows, and individuals affected by poverty, illness, or violence, fostering community well-being and empowerment.

Popular Causes

Let's know about charity causes around the world

Poverty Alleviation

Poverty remains one of the most pressing global challenges. Charities dedicated to poverty alleviation focus on providing basic necessities like food, clean water, and shelter, as well as offering education and job training to help individuals break the cycle of poverty. These initiatives also advocate for fair economic policies and social safety nets to ensure long-term financial stability for vulnerable populations.

Health and Disease Prevention

Health-focused charities aim to improve access to healthcare services, promote disease prevention, and provide support for those affected by chronic illnesses, epidemics, and disabilities. They work on the front lines of health crises such as HIV/AIDS, malaria, and tuberculosis, as well as provide support for mental health and wellness programs. These organizations also fund medical research to find cures for various diseases and improve treatment options worldwide.

Child Protection and Education

Charities focused on child protection and education work to ensure that children are safe, healthy, and able to receive an education. These organizations fight against child labor, trafficking, and abuse while promoting access to quality education, healthcare, and nutrition. They aim to give every child the opportunity to grow and thrive in a supportive environment.

Environmental Conservation

Environmental conservation charities focus on preserving natural habitats, fighting climate change, and protecting endangered species. They work on projects that promote sustainability, reduce pollution, and conserve water resources. These organizations also educate the public on the importance of eco-friendly practices and advocate for policy changes to address environmental challenges.

INI Director Had the Privilege to Be Among the Panelists at the National Disaster Risk Reduction Symposium in Kilifi County

Inclusive Network Initiatives (INI) is proud to announce that our Director was honored to be a panelist at the National Disaster Risk Reduction Symposium, held in Kilifi County. This pivotal event brought together experts, policymakers, and stakeholders to discuss strategies for improving disaster response efforts, particularly ensuring that persons with disabilities (PWDs) are not left behind in these critical times. A Focus on Inclusion in Disaster Response Disasters, whether natural or man-made, have a disproportionate impact on vulnerable populations, and persons with disabilities are often among the most affected. The symposium highlighted the urgent need for inclusive disaster preparedness and response strategies that consider the unique needs of PWDs. Our Director participated in a panel discussion that centered around how we can better integrate PWDs into disaster risk management frameworks, ensuring their safety, dignity, and well-being during emergencies.
